1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Grafide takes a minimal approach to cookies — we use only what is necessary for the platform to function. We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking cookies.

2. What We Use

Grafide uses localStorage — browser-based storage — rather than traditional cookies for most session data. This means data stays on your device and is not transmitted in HTTP headers.

Storage KeyPurposeTypeExpires
grafide_token Stores your JWT authentication token to keep you logged in localStorage 24 hours (token expiry)
grafide_user Stores your name, email, and role for UI display localStorage Until you sign out
grafide_progress_* Caches your lesson progress locally for faster loading localStorage Until cleared or synced

3. Storage Types Explained

We use localStorage rather than cookies. The key difference:

  • localStorage data is stored entirely on your device
  • It is never automatically sent to the server in HTTP headers
  • It persists until explicitly cleared or you sign out
  • It cannot be accessed by other websites

This approach is more privacy-friendly than traditional cookie-based sessions because your session data never travels with every HTTP request — only when you explicitly make API calls.

4. Third-Party Content

Some lessons embed YouTube videos. When a YouTube video is embedded and played, YouTube may set its own cookies on your device in accordance with Google's privacy policy. Grafide does not control these cookies.

External resource links on lesson pages open in a new tab. Visiting those external sites may result in those sites setting their own cookies. Grafide is not responsible for third-party cookie practices.

YouTube videos are embedded using standard iframes. If you are concerned about YouTube cookies, you can watch linked videos directly on YouTube instead.

5. Your Control

You can clear Grafide's localStorage data at any time:

  • Sign out — clears your token and user data from localStorage
  • Browser settings — clear site data for grafide.com in your browser's developer tools or privacy settings
  • Private/Incognito mode — localStorage is cleared when you close the window

Clearing localStorage will sign you out and reset locally cached progress. Your server-side progress and certificates are not affected.

To manage YouTube cookies, refer to Google's Privacy Policy.
